# Customer Retention & Revenue Risk Analysis

## Objective

Identify high-value customers at risk of churn and prioritize retention efforts based on revenue impact.

**Dataset**

    - 525,461 cleaned transactions

    - ~4,300 customers

    - 2,893 repeat customers

    - Churn defined as 90 days of inactivity

## Key Findings

**1 Revenue Is Highly Concentrated**

    - 42.9% of customers generate **52% of total revenue**

    - 9.4% generate **20.8% of revenue**

    - 5 customers (0.12%) generate **12.25% of revenue**

**Implication:** Revenue risk is concentrated. Losing a small number of customers can materially impact total revenue.


**2 Churn Rate (Repeat Customers)**

    - 21.9% classified as churned

    - Churn driven primarily by declining purchase frequency (not basket size)

    - Return rate slightly higher among churned customers


**3 Revenue Leakage from Churned Segments**

| **Segment** |	**Revenue Share** |
--------------------------
| Churned Low Value Mass Shoppers |	6.41% |
------------------------------------------
| Churned Versatile Mid-Tier | 2.46% |
-------------------------------------
| Churned One-Off Buyers | 5%+ combined |


**Key Insight:** Win-back strategies targeting historically valuable customers provide measurable ROI.


## Retention Priority Framework

**Tier 1 – Protect Revenue Core**

    - Active high-frequency and mid-tier customers

    - Implement 60–90 day recency triggers

**Tier 2 – High-ROI Win-Back**

    - Churned mid-tier & mass shoppers

    - Personalized reactivation campaigns

**Tier 3 – Conversion Strategy**

    - High-value one-off buyers

    - Incentivize second purchase behavior

**Tier 4 – Low Priority**

    - Low-value one-time customers

    - Minimal retention investment


## Business Recommendations

    - Deploy recency-based churn alerts

    - Monitor top 1% customers individually

    - Prioritize frequency-building incentives

    - Experimentally test retention campaigns by segment
